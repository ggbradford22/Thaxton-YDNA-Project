Thaxton YDNA Project

The Thaxton YDNA Project is a genetic genealogy initiative designed to explore, verify and document the paternal lineage of men bearing the surname Thaxton and its known spelling variations (such as Thaxten, Thackston, Thaxtun, Thackstone, etc.). Because YDNA is passed down virtually unchanged from father to son, it serves as a powerful tool for tracing male ancestry and uncovering connections between families that may not be evident through traditional records alone.

The core goals of the Thaxton YDNA Project are:

  • To identify and connect distinct Thaxton paternal lineages.
  • To support and enhance traditional genealogical research.
  • To map the geographic and historical origins of Thaxton lineages.
  • To preserve and share YDNA results in a collaborative, privacy-conscious setting.
  • To encourage participation among Thaxton-surnamed males worldwide.

By combining YDNA testing with historical and genealogical research, the Thaxton YDNA Project aims to build a clearer, scientifically grounded picture of the Thaxton surname's origins and evolution across generations.
